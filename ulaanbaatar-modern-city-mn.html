Modern Ulaanbaatar – Mongolia’s Capital City Guide

Ulaanbaatar is home to nearly 1 million people out of Mongolia’s total population of 3.5 million. Despite its relatively small population, traffic congestion in winter can take 2–4 hours. In summer, traffic becomes much lighter and more comfortable for travelers.

Main Attractions

  • Sukhbaatar Square
  • Gandan Monastery
  • Zaisan Hill
  • Chinggis Khan Museum
  • National Museum

Shopping & Cashmere

Ulaanbaatar offers international brands and famous Mongolian cashmere.

Top brands include Gobi Cashmere and Evseg Cashmere.

Silver Craft Culture

Mongolians traditionally use silver bowls for tea and food. The most valuable type, “Noyon Sevrei,” can cost between $1000–$3000.

Winter in Ulaanbaatar

Ulaanbaatar is one of the coldest capitals in the world, reaching -40°C. Fur clothing like sheepskin, fox, and wolf fur is commonly used.

Nightlife

Enjoy live music at Hard Rock Cafe, Broadway, and rooftop lounges. The city stays active until 3–4 AM.

Transportation

Use taxi apps like UBCab and ABA. Public transport is cheap but requires a card.
