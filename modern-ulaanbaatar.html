Introduction

Mongolia’s young and energetic capital

Ulaanbaatar is a city where traditional culture and modern life meet. Despite Mongolia’s relatively small population, the capital is lively, fast-changing, and full of places that interest international travelers.

📍
City of contrasts: Buddhist monasteries, museums, luxury malls, rooftop lounges, and busy streets all exist side by side.
🗣️
Friendly atmosphere: In the city center, many locals have a basic understanding of English and are generally welcoming.
🧳
Good base for travelers: Ulaanbaatar is the best place to prepare for countryside trips, buy essentials, exchange money, and explore Mongolian culture.
Top Places

Main attractions in Ulaanbaatar

These are the key places most travelers should see while spending time in the city.

Sukhbaatar Square

The city’s main central square and one of the best starting points for exploring Ulaanbaatar.

Gandan Monastery

One of the most important Buddhist monasteries in Mongolia and a must-see for cultural visitors.

Zaisan Hill

A popular viewpoint where travelers can enjoy wide city views, especially at sunset.

Chinggis Khaan Museum

A modern museum that helps visitors understand the history of the Mongol Empire and Mongolia’s rulers.

National Museum of Mongolia

A strong introduction to Mongolian history, nomadic life, traditional dress, and daily culture.

Bogd Khaan Palace Museum

A historic palace museum showing royal life, religion, and interesting artifacts from Mongolia’s past.

Shopping

Shopping, malls and Mongolian cashmere

Ulaanbaatar offers major shopping centers where travelers can find international brands, restaurants, cafés, and useful travel items. Popular places include Shangri-La Mall, the State Department Store, and Hunnu Mall.

Mongolian cashmere is one of the city’s most famous shopping highlights. Visitors often look for scarves, sweaters, coats, and gifts from well-known brands such as Gobi Cashmere and Evseg Cashmere.

Cashmere is one of the best quality souvenirs to bring home from Mongolia.

Traditional Craft

Silver craft and silver bowl culture

Mongolian metalworkers have long been respected for producing beautiful handmade silver items. Silver bowls hold a special place in Mongolian homes, and many people believe eating or drinking from silver is good for health.

One of the most valuable types is the Noyon Sevrei style silver bowl. Depending on size, details, and craftsmanship, it can cost roughly $1,000–$3,000.

Travelers can also find silver earrings, pendants, rings, bracelets, and smaller silver souvenirs in city shops and gift stores.

Winter Life

Winter in Ulaanbaatar

Ulaanbaatar is one of the coldest capitals in the world. Winter temperatures can fall below -30°C and at times reach around -40°C. For many visitors, such cold weather is almost impossible to imagine without proper protection.

This is why Mongolians have traditionally valued fur, sheepskin, and other warm natural materials. In winter you will often see sheepskin coats, fur-lined clothing, leather boots, and garments made from fox, wolf, marmot, rabbit, and other warm materials.

These items are not only for style. They are part of surviving the severe winter climate comfortably.

Traveler tip: If you visit Mongolia in winter, warm boots, gloves, a heavy coat, thick socks, and layers are essential.
Nightlife

After dark in Ulaanbaatar

Ulaanbaatar’s nightlife has become more active in recent years. Travelers can enjoy live music, bars, rooftop venues, and lounges around the city. Places such as Hard Rock Cafe, Broadway, and other live-music venues can be enjoyable evening choices.

Some parts of the city remain active until around 3–4 AM, giving Ulaanbaatar a much livelier night atmosphere than many first-time visitors expect.

As in any city, it is best to choose reliable venues, avoid unclear night spots, and use taxi apps to return safely.

Transport

Getting around the city

Taxi apps: UBCab and ABA are among the more reliable options for travelers.
Public transport: Cheap and useful, but it usually requires a transport card that can be topped up near bus stops.
Scooters and mopeds: Common in summer, but many use QPay payment, which can be difficult for foreign visitors.
Traffic note: Winter traffic can be heavy, while summer is generally easier for moving around the city.
